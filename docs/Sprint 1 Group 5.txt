Background Research Recommendation: Image Teams 
Before writing any code, each member of the team should spend time researching the following topics. Understanding these concepts will directly inform how you load your data, what you look for during EDA, and how you will preprocess in Sprint 2.
What to Research
* How image datasets are typically structured in folders and how to load them using Python libraries like PIL, OpenCV, or torchvision
* What image normalization means and why pixel values are scaled before feeding images into a model
* What data augmentation is and why it helps a model generalize better
* What transfer learning is at a high level, specifically what a pretrained CNN like MobileNet or ResNet already knows and why that matters for your project
* What class imbalance means in an image dataset and why it can hurt model performance
What Your EDA Notebook Should Show
* Class distribution bar chart showing how many images exist per label. This immediately tells you whether the dataset is balanced or whether some classes have far more examples than others.
* Sample image grid displaying several example images from each class so the team can visually understand what the model will be learning from. Look for variation in lighting, angle, background, and quality.
* Image dimension distribution showing the range of heights and widths across the dataset. Most real world datasets have inconsistent sizes and you need to know this before preprocessing.
* Pixel intensity histogram showing the distribution of pixel brightness values across the dataset or across a sample. This tells you whether normalization is necessary and how extreme the range is.
* Corrupted or missing file check documenting any images that fail to load, are unusually small, or appear blank. Even a small number of bad files can cause training errors later.
* Written summary at the end of the notebook describing what the team observed, what challenges are anticipated, and what preprocessing steps will likely be needed going into Sprint 2.
